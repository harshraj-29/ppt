Abstract

Diabetic retinopathy (DR) is a major cause of visual impairment and blindness in diabetic patients globally. Early recognition and correct diagnosis are needed to prevent the progression of DR. This research presents an automated detection and classification approach to DR using a combination of deep learning techniques and advanced image analysis of retinal fundus images.

We implement Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) based on ResNet-50 architecture to extract hierarchical features and classify retinal images across five severity stages of DR. Image preprocessing methods including Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (CLAHE) and noise reduction were applied to enhance model performance.

The system achieved an overall accuracy of 93.1%, with sensitivity of 91.7% and specificity of 93.8%. Testing on 1,500 preprocessed fundus images from EyePACS, Messidor, and DIARETDB1 datasets demonstrated robust performance. The average inference time of 0.18 seconds per image confirms real-time screening capability.

Model Interpretability: Grad-CAM Visualization

Understanding Model Decision-Making Process

  • Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) used for visual explanations

Detected Features
  • Microaneurysms: Small red dots indicating early DR
  • Hemorrhages: Bleeding in retinal layers
  • Hard Exudates: Lipid deposits (yellow/white lesions)
  • Soft Exudates: Cotton-wool spots
  • Neovascularization: Abnormal blood vessel growth
Clinical Validation
Grad-CAM visualizations confirm that the CNN correctly focuses on pathological regions consistent with ophthalmological diagnostic criteria. Heat maps demonstrate attention to lesion areas in moderate and proliferative DR images, validating the model's learning of clinically meaningful features rather than spurious correlations.
